Our Story

The Art
of Glass

For over fifteen years, Luminara has been at the intersection of ancient craft and contemporary design — creating glass artworks that capture light, hold memory, and endure through generations.

A Family Workshop Born from Passion

Our techniques trace their lineage to Venetian glassblowing traditions introduced to the Indian subcontinent centuries ago, reinterpreted through a distinctly Indian aesthetic sensibility — one that finds beauty in the interplay of colour, light, and cultural symbolism.

Today, Luminara's studio employs over a hundred artisans, each trained for years in the ancient methods of gathering, blowing, shaping, and annealing. Every piece that leaves our studio bears the signature of the craftsperson who created it.

How We Create

The Making of a
Luminara Piece

01
✏️

Design & Conception

Every piece begins as a collaboration between designer and artisan. Sketches are refined, colours are mixed, and proportions are tested in miniature before work begins at the furnace.

02
🔥

The Gathering

Silica sand, soda ash, and lime are melted at 1,400°C in our wood-fired furnaces. The glassblower gathers the molten mass on an iron blowpipe — a moment that requires years of intuition to perform correctly.

03
🤲

Shaping & Blowing

Through a rhythmic sequence of blowing, rotating, and shaping with ancient cherry-wood tools, the glassblower coaxes the incandescent material into its intended form. The glass must be constantly reheated to remain workable.

04
✨

Annealing & Finishing

The finished piece is placed in an annealing oven at 480°C and cooled slowly over twelve hours. This relieves internal stress in the glass. After annealing, each piece is inspected, polished, signed, and photographed.
